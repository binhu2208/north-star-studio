# Controlled Burn Reference Board

**Character:** Ember  
**Phase:** 5 — Controlled Burn  
**Purpose:** Visual direction for Phase 5 card art (Cards 17-20)

---

## Phase Arc Overview

Ember's journey from chaotic fire (Phases 1-2) → destructive consequences (Phase 3-4) → **mastered control (Phase 5)** → sustainable flame (Phase 6).

Phase 5 is the pivotal turn: where anger transforms from a liability into a tool. Visual language should communicate **restraint, precision, and contained power**.

### Lighting Direction

- **Source:** Localized heat (forge mouth, coal bed, ember core)
- **Style:** Directional with strong falloff, not ambient orange wash
- **Shadows:** Deep and surrounding — fire feels contained
- **Highlights:** Dense and selective — only at hottest points
- **Avoid:** All-over glow, magical halos, strobe-like flare

---

## Mood & Lighting References

### Emotional Tone

- **Mastered intensity** — power held in reserve
- **Steadiness** — banked coals, not explosions
- **Utility** — fire as tool, not weapon
- **Tension without rupture** — pressurized but not about to detonate

### Lighting Approach

1. **One dominant heat source** per card
2. **Clear focal zone** with surrounding darkness
3. **Restrained highlight** — white/yellow only at peak heat
4. **Practical light** — feels industrial, weighty, believable
5. **Heat bloom** — subtle shimmer, no divine radiance

### Reference Photography

- Moody bonfire/campfire shots with dark surroundings
- Blacksmith forge work — localized orange glow
- Tempered steel — blue-brown oxidation colors
- Charcoal/coal beds — dense red-orange under dark crust

Phase 5 should feel distinct from earlier chaos:

## Composition Guidelines

### Do

- ✅ One contained heat focal point
- ✅ Mid-close compositions for texture readability
- ✅ Stable, anchored shapes (triangular, horizontal)
- ✅ Framing elements (anvils, stone rings, metal braces)
- ✅ Dark negative space for contrast

### Don't

- ❌ Giant background fire spectacle
- ❌ Upward rebirth poses (drifts to Phase 6)
- ❌ Wing-like flame shapes
- ❌ Edge-to-edge sparks/smoke
- ❌ Generic red-orange gradients without material
- ❌ Screaming faces or explosive action
- ❌ Busy backgrounds competing with focal point

## Card-Specific Notes

### Bonfire (Card 17)
- Compact upward flame, not sprawling
- Ember calm keeper, not aggressor
- Warm amber, comfortable not violent

### Forge (Card 18)
- Strong diagonal action
- Sparks follow hammer arc
- Hot iron: orange, yellow-white, steel black

### Controlled Burn (Card 19)
- Clean enclosed shape (circle, ring, arc)
- Ember composed, upright, not panicking
- Most graphic/layout-focused of the batch

### Tempered Steel (Card 20)
- Steel form dominates (blade, shield, plate)
- Hot interior glow vs. cool hardened surface
- Least flame-dominant card in batch

## Summary

Phase 5 Controlled Burn = **disciplined heat, tempered force, strategic control**

Key visual drivers:
- Contained fire systems (hearth, forge, brazier)
- Material texture (soot, iron, scorched stone)
- Localized lighting with dark surroundings
- Stable, intentional compositions
- Palette: orange-red with brass/iron/gold accents

This phase bridges the gap between destructive fire and sustainable flame — Ember has learned to use what once nearly destroyed them.
