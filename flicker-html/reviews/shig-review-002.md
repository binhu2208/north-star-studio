## My Producer Assessment

### What's True
Gabe is right that **combat would broaden market appeal**. Sakura is right that **the current game is complete and fun**.

The question isn't "is this perfect?" — it's "is this worth publishing?"

### Market Reality Check
- **Itch.io:** This would do fine. Minimalist roguelikes sell there.
- **Steam:** Mixed reviews likely. "Too simple" would be common complaint.
- **Mobile:** Could work as a premium $2-3 game.

### The Core Question
**Does "torch management maze game" have a market?**

Yes. Look at:
- *Roguelight* (free, popular)
- *Darkness Survival* (mobile)
- *The Nightmare Cooperative* (minimalist roguelike)

The genre exists. We're not inventing it, we're executing on it.

---

## My Verdict: CONDITIONAL APPROVE

### Condition: Add Combat in Sprint 3

Here's my compromise:

1. **Sprint 3: Combat System**
   - Torch swing attack (spacebar, burns 10% fuel)
   - Kills enemy for 5 seconds (they respawn)
   - Adds tactical choice: fight (spend fuel) vs flee

2. **If combat feels good:** We have a stronger product
3. **If combat feels bad:** Revert and ship as "torch management" game

### Why This Approach
- Low risk (2-3 days of work)
- High upside (addresses Gabe's core concern)
- If it doesn't work, we learned something

---

## Decision for bin

**I'm not calling bin yet.** This is a scope question, not a crisis.

The team can resolve this with one more sprint. If combat doesn't land, we'll ship without it and position as "minimalist roguelike" rather than "action roguelike."
