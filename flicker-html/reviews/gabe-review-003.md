## 1. Combat System Assessment

### Implementation Analysis

| Feature | Implementation | Market Impact |
|---------|---------------|---------------|
| **Attack Input** | SPACE key | ✅ Intuitive, standard binding |
| **Attack Cost** | 10% fuel per swing | ✅ Creates tension, forces decisions |
| **Directional** | Faces player direction | ✅ Requires positioning skill |
| **Visual Feedback** | Flame burst emoji effect | ⚠️ Functional but needs polish |
| **Audio Feedback** | Synthesized whoosh sound | ✅ Improves game feel |
| **Enemy Death** | Kill text + despawn | ✅ Satisfying feedback |
| **Respawn Timer** | 5 seconds | ✅ Keeps pressure, prevents camping |

### The Critical Question: Does Combat Work?

**Yes. Here's why:**

The combat creates a **genuine risk/reward tension** that was missing:

1. **Fight vs Flee Decisions**
   - Low fuel? You can't afford to attack — must run
   - High fuel? You can clear the enemy for breathing room
   - Enemy blocking exit? Spend fuel or find another path

2. **Skill Expression**
   - Positioning matters (attack in front of you)
   - Timing matters (enemy respawns — no permanent safety)
   - Resource management matters (every swing costs 10%)

3. **Build Variety Integration**
   - Speed Boost builds: Outrun enemies, attack only when necessary
   - Efficiency builds: Can afford more attacks, aggressive playstyle
   - Bright Torch builds: Better positioning knowledge, tactical advantage

**Flicker is now ready for Early Access.**

1. **Combat creates the core decision loop** — Every encounter is "fight (spend fuel) or flee (risk damage/darkness)"

## 8. Final Word

Sprint 3 delivers on the promise. The combat system transforms Flicker from a walking simulator with a timer into a **genuine tactical roguelike**.

The torch mechanic — Flicker's golden ticket — finally has the support system it needed. Combat gives players **agency** in the darkness. Every attack is a choice with consequences. Every respawn keeps the pressure on.

**Is it perfect?** No. Art is still emoji placeholders. Audio is minimal. Enemy variety is limited.

**Is it market-ready?** Yes. The core experience is fun, unique, and replayable.

**My recommendation:** 
- APPROVE for Early Access
- Greenlight pixel art implementation
- Target EA launch in 2-4 weeks with art + audio polish

The potential I saw in Review #001 is now realized. Flicker has become a game worth playing — and worth selling.
