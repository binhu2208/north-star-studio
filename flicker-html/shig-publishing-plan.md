## Executive Summary

Based on Gabe's market research and our Phase 1 deliverables, I've created three publishing timeline options for **Flicker** (formerly Torch Roguelike). Each option balances scope, quality, and time-to-market differently.

**My Recommendation:** **Balanced Timeline (14 months)** — It gives us enough time to deliver a polished, competitive product while maintaining momentum and managing burn rate for a small first-time team.

## Recommendation: Balanced Timeline

### Why Balanced Fits North Star Studio

1. **First-Game Realities:** As a new studio, we need time to learn. 14 months gives us buffer to make mistakes and recover.

2. **Market Positioning:** Gabe's research shows the roguelike market rewards quality over speed. A rushed 8-month product risks being buried. A 22-month product risks never shipping.

3. **Team Sustainability:** 3 people working sustainably beats 2 people burning out or 5 people draining budget.

4. **Competitive Analysis:** Into the Breach took ~4 years but that was Subset's second game. FTL took ~2 years. Our 14-month timeline is aggressive but achievable for our scope.

5. **Financial Reality:** $45k-65k is a significant but manageable investment. It gives us runway without requiring external funding.

6. **Wishlist Math:** Industry standard is 1 sale per 10-20 wishlists at launch. Our 20,000 wishlist target = 1,000-2,000 day 1 sales, with long tail potential. This is realistic for a quality indie roguelike.

### Critical Success Factors

1. **Vertical Slice Validation (Month 4):** If the core loop isn't fun by the vertical slice, we cut losses or pivot.

2. **Steam Page Performance (Month 5):** If we're not hitting wishlist targets, we adjust marketing or scope before it's too late.

3. **Demo Reception (Month 6):** Demo completion rate should be 40%+. Lower means we're not communicating the hook well.

4. **Beta Feedback (Month 10):** Content creator enthusiasm at beta is a strong launch predictor. If they're not excited, we delay and polish.

### Risk Mitigation

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Scope creep | Monthly "cut list" review; features must justify their cost |
| Team member loss | Cross-training; John documents everything |
| Budget overrun | 15% buffer built in; monthly budget reviews |
| Low wishlist conversion | Early and frequent community engagement; adjust messaging |
| Delayed launch | 1-month buffer built into timeline; features are cuttable |

# Appendix: Gabe's Market Research Summary

*The following is a condensed version of Gabe's market analysis that informed these timelines. For the full report, see `/studio/projects/flicker/gabe-market-report.md`.*

## Key Market Insights

### Roguelike Market Size
- **Market Value:** $1.5B (2024), projected $3.2B by 2033 (9.5% CAGR)
- **PC Indie Segment:** ~20% of all action games on Steam are roguelikes/lites
- **Turn-Based Niche:** Underserved — Into the Breach and FTL are main references, both 5+ years old

### Competitive Landscape
| Game | Peak Concurrent | What Players Complain About |
|------|-----------------|---------------------------|
| Slay the Spire 2 | 282,000+ | "Samey runs", difficulty spikes, RNG frustration |
| Hades II | 113,000+ | Repetitive environments, forced combat |
| Into the Breach | 12,000+ | Too puzzle-like, lack of enemy variety |
| Balatro | 112,000+ | Gets stale, lack of meta-progression |

### Flicker's Differentiation
1. **Real-time resource in turn-based game** — No competitor has this
2. **15-30 minute runs** — Addresses "time commitment" complaint
3. **Pause-anywhere** — Turn-based = natural accessibility
4. **No grindy unlocks** — Respects player time
5. **Clear visual information** — Light/dark intuitive language

### Development Benchmarks
| Game | Dev Time | Team Size | Notes |
|------|----------|-----------|-------|
| FTL | ~2 years | 2 people | Subset's first game |
| Into the Breach | ~4 years | 2 people | Subset's second game |
| Slay the Spire | ~2 years | 2 people | Mega Crit debut |
| Hades | ~3 years | 20+ people | Supergiant (experienced) |
| Vampire Survivors | ~1 year | 1 person | Exception, not rule |

### Timeline Justification
Our **14-month Balanced timeline** compares favorably:
- Faster than experienced teams (Into the Breach: 4 years, Hades: 3 years)
- Comparable to successful debut games (FTL: 2 years, STS: 2 years)
- Accounts for our small team (3 people vs. Supergiant's 20+)

### Player Demand Validation
- Reddit r/gameideas post describing nearly this exact concept: **133 upvotes, enthusiastic comments**
- r/roguelikes community: **Active, engaged, constantly seeking new mechanics**
- Key quote: "Very minimalistic game in which you carry a single element: a torch... Turn-based game, but the fire of the torch slowly goes out in real-time"

### Price Point Analysis
- **Target:** $14.99-$19.99
- **Comparable:** Into the Breach ($14.99), FTL ($9.99, older), Monster Train ($24.99)
- **Positioning:** Mid-tier indie pricing; not budget, not premium

### Platform Strategy
1. **PC (Steam) first** — Roguelikes live and die on Steam
2. **Switch second** — Roguelikes perform exceptionally well on Switch
3. **Mobile potential** — Turn-based + short runs = perfect for mobile

### Success Metrics to Track
| Metric | Target | Why It Matters |
|--------|--------|----------------|
| Wishlist conversion | 10%+ | Industry standard for quality indies |
| Demo completion rate | 40%+ | Indicates hook is working |
| Average session length | 20-30 min | Validates "short run" design |
| Playtest NPS | 50+ | Fun factor validation |
| Content creator interest | 50+ at beta | Launch visibility predictor |

### Risk Assessment (from Gabe)
| Risk | Confidence | Mitigation |
|------|------------|------------|
| Torch mechanic isn't fun | HIGH | Prototype in 2 weeks, test with 10 players |
| Too similar to Roguelight | MEDIUM | Different genre (tactical vs platformer) |
| Roguelike market saturation | MEDIUM | Unique mechanic + QOL focus |
| Scope creep | HIGH | Strict 12-month MVP, cut features aggressively |
